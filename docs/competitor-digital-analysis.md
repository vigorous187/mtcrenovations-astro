# The digital bar for Burlington-Hamilton-Oakville renovation contractors — and how MTC can clear it

**MTC Renovations faces a market where the top competitors have built substantial digital moats, but the gap is closable with the right strategy.** The leading contractors in this tri-city corridor — Domilya Group, Newfore, and Rose Building Group — command Instagram followings of 10,000–22,000 and post 3–6 times per week, while MTC sits at 182 followers with 42 posts. The good news: almost none of these competitors are executing a complete digital strategy, and several high-impact content types remain virtually untouched. This report profiles all 10 top-performing competitors, identifies exactly what's working, and maps a concrete path for MTC to differentiate.

---

## The 10 contractors setting the standard

The following companies represent the strongest digital presences among residential renovation contractors serving Burlington, Hamilton, and Oakville. They're ranked by overall digital footprint — a composite of social following, website quality, portfolio depth, and review profile.

### 1. Newfore Inc. — The volume king

**Website:** newfore.com | **Instagram:** @newforeinc (22,000 followers, 2,363 posts) | **Facebook:** facebook.com/newforeinc (144,400 likes)

Newfore dominates on sheer output. Their Instagram strategy leans heavily on **personality-driven Reels** — motivational quotes, humorous contractor culture content, and relatable crew moments — rather than polished portfolio shots. This approach drives algorithmic reach to non-followers and has built the largest social following in the market. Their website features ~30 residential projects organized as individual case studies with dedicated pages, category filtering (kitchens, bathrooms, basements, additions, garden suites, commercial), and claims of 200+ completed projects. They offer a **client management app** where clients track scheduling, material selections, and progress photos in real time.

**What they do well with photos:** Volume and variety. Their content calendar never stops, and they mix project photography with personality content that feeds the algorithm. Each portfolio project gets its own page with scope descriptions and multiple images.

**What's getting engagement:** Personality-first Reels (humor, relatable contractor life) dramatically outperform their portfolio posts. A casual Facebook post asking "What's the funniest thing you believed on your first day?" pulled **96 reactions** — showing their audience engages with culture, not just craftsmanship.

**Weakness to exploit:** Their review profile is polarized — **4.5 stars on Google with 117 reviews**, a BBB F rating, and some scathing complaints about quality and communication. Their photos, while abundant, lack the professional polish of competitors like Domilya or Zzone. The 144K Facebook likes figure is anomalously high for a local contractor and may reflect paid acquisition.

## What photos actually win in this market

Across all 10 competitors, clear patterns emerge about which visual content drives engagement and builds trust.

**Before-and-after transformations dominate.** Every data point — from industry research to competitor performance — confirms this. Carousels showing before → progress → after sequences average **10% higher engagement** than single photos. Yet surprisingly, only **3 of the 10 competitors** (Bradburn, IntegriMark, and Domilya) prominently feature before/after content. Most show only finished results. This is a massive gap.

**Finished room photography is table stakes, not a differentiator.** All 10 competitors post finished rooms. The ones that stand out pair finished photos with context: Domilya's Houzz Q&A about specific products, Wynwood's design challenge narratives, Bradburn's case study storytelling. A beautiful kitchen photo alone generates likes; a beautiful kitchen photo with the story of "what this room looked like 8 weeks ago and why the homeowner chose this layout" generates saves, shares, and inquiries.

**Personality and crew content punches above its weight.** Newfore built the largest following in the market (22K) primarily through personality-driven Reels — not project showcases. Rose's founder Matthew Rose is visible throughout their brand. Wynwood's Lilla features in personal branding Reels. The contractors who show faces outperform those who show only rooms.

**Video format is now mandatory.** Reels receive algorithmic preference on Instagram and reach non-followers far more effectively than static posts. Bradburn's AI-glasses first-person video series, Zzone's 3D rendering reveals, and Newfore's humorous Reels all demonstrate that video content drives discovery. Static-only accounts like IntegriMark (672 followers despite 280 posts) show the penalty for ignoring video.

## Presentation techniques worth copying

Several competitors use specific techniques that MTC should adopt immediately.

**Wynwood's named project approach** transforms generic portfolio entries into memorable narratives. Instead of "Kitchen Renovation #14," labeling it "Project Williamson — a dining retreat adjacent to a beautifully designed two-tone custom kitchen" creates emotional resonance and makes the portfolio browsable as a storybook. Each project description frames the design challenge and the homeowner's needs.

**Zzone's 3D rendering → finished photo pairing** creates a unique before/after that doesn't require ugly "before" photos. Showing a photorealistic 3D render alongside the finished room demonstrates design capability and builds confidence in the planning process. This is especially powerful for Instagram carousels.

**Bradburn's case study format** wraps project photos inside narrative arcs — the client's initial frustration, the design vision, construction challenges, and the reveal. Combined with embedded video testimonials, this transforms a portfolio page into a persuasion machine.

**Domilya's extensive photo depth** (25–40 images per whole-home project) lets prospects self-qualify by spending significant time exploring. This extended engagement signals to Google that the page is high-value, improving SEO. It also answers detailed questions prospects have about finish quality, consistency, and attention to detail.

**Rose's HGTV badge strategy** demonstrates how third-party credibility markers (Baeumler Approved, HGTV featured, RenoMark) displayed prominently on every page and social bio convert casual browsers into serious inquirers. These badges appear in their Instagram bio, website footer, and project pages.

### Caption and hashtag strategies

The best-performing captions in this market share three characteristics: they **open with a hook** ("SWIPE to see this 1980s kitchen transform"), they **include specific details** (materials, timeline, square footage, budget range), and they **end with a question** that invites comments ("Which feature would you keep forever?"). Newfore's casual, conversational tone works for reach; Domilya's detailed, professional tone works for conversion. MTC should blend both — conversational hooks with substantive detail.

For hashtags, the effective local strategy uses **20–25 tags** mixing three tiers: broad reach tags (#homerenovation, #beforeandafter, #kitchenremodel), local geo tags (#BurlingtonOntario, #HamiltonRenovation, #OakvilleHomes, #HaltonRegion), and niche targeting tags (#modernfarmhousekitchen, #basementtransformation, #canadiancontractor). Every post should include location tagging.

## What nobody is doing — MTC's differentiation opportunities

Despite the competitive intensity, there are **five significant gaps** that no contractor in this market is filling well.

**1. Interactive before/after sliders on the website.** Not a single competitor uses a slider or swipe-comparison tool for before/after photos. Domilya labels files "before" and "after" but dumps them in the same gallery. Bradburn shows before/after in case studies but without interactive comparison. An interactive slider on every project page — where visitors drag to reveal the transformation — would be an immediate, visible differentiator and dramatically increase time on site.

**2. Homeowner journey documentation.** No competitor is telling the full homeowner story. They show the house, not the humans. A series following a real client from "we hate our kitchen" through design consultation, demolition day, construction milestones, material selection moments, and the emotional reveal would be unprecedented in this market. Film the homeowner's reaction when they see the finished space for the first time. This content is the most shareable format in renovation marketing and nobody locally is producing it.

**3. Transparent cost content.** Only Zzone publishes renovation cost guides. No competitor shows actual project budgets alongside portfolio photos. Creating content that says "This Burlington kitchen renovation: $65,000. Here's exactly what that bought" would break through the industry's cost-secrecy norm and generate enormous organic reach. Homeowners are desperate for real pricing information.

**4. TikTok presence.** Only one competitor (Pro-Fit Construction in Oakville) has a TikTok account. For a platform that skews toward first-time homebuyers and younger homeowners — the exact demographic entering the renovation market — this is a wide-open channel. Short-form demolition videos, satisfying tile installations, and dramatic before/after reveals are among the most viral content categories on TikTok.

**5. Consolidated brand identity.** MTC currently splits content between @mtcrenovations (construction progress) and @michaelthehomebuyer (before/after). This fragments the audience, confuses the brand, and means the most engagement-driving content type (before/after) lives on a separate account. **Merging all renovation content under @mtcrenovations — especially the before/after transformations — is the single highest-impact change MTC can make.** Every competitor keeps their best content on their primary business account.

### Additional gaps MTC can fill

The blog at mtcrenovations.ca exists but has **zero indexed posts**. Competitors like Bradburn, Zzone, and Newfore actively publish SEO-optimized blog content (renovation cost guides, design trend articles, "what to expect" educational pieces). With 30 location pages already built, adding just 2 blog posts per month with localized keywords ("kitchen renovation cost Burlington 2026") would compound MTC's search visibility rapidly.

No competitor maintains organized **Instagram Story Highlights** categorized by room type (Kitchens, Bathrooms, Basements) with curated project mini-tours. This is free portfolio infrastructure that lives permanently on the profile.

MTC's website photography appears sourced from **real estate virtual tour photography** (MLS-style images with virtual tour filenames). Investing in **dedicated renovation portfolio photography** — with same-angle before/after shots, detail close-ups, and consistent editing style — would immediately elevate the perceived quality of work to match competitors like Domilya and Zzone.

## The 90-day plan to close the gap

MTC's current digital position is significantly behind the competition on social media but has a solid foundation (modern website, strong Google rating, extensive local SEO). Here's the priority sequence:

**Weeks 1–2: Foundation fixes.** Consolidate all before/after content onto @mtcrenovations — stop directing it to @michaelthehomebuyer. Fix the brand name inconsistency (Facebook uses "MTC Renovation" singular). Replace virtual-tour-sourced portfolio images with properly photographed renovation shots. Create Instagram Story Highlights for each service category.

**Weeks 3–6: Content engine activation.** Begin posting 4 times per week: Monday (before/after carousel), Wednesday (progress Reel or Story), Friday (finished room with story caption), plus one rotating slot for crew content, tips, or testimonials. Start documenting every active project with same-angle before photos on day one. Publish 2 blog posts targeting "renovation cost [city] 2026" keywords.

**Weeks 7–12: Differentiation deployment.** Install interactive before/after sliders on every portfolio page. Launch a "homeowner journey" video series following one client from consultation to reveal. Create the market's first transparent cost-breakdown content. Establish a TikTok presence with short-form demolition and reveal content. Target 500+ Instagram followers by day 90.

MTC's **4.9 Google rating** is already competitive with the best in the market. The website's modern design and 30 location pages provide strong infrastructure. The gap is almost entirely in **content volume, content variety, and social proof** — all of which are addressable through consistent execution rather than major investment. The competitors have set a clear bar. Now MTC knows exactly where it is.
