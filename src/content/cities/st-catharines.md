## Renovating in the Garden City

St. Catharines earned its "Garden City" nickname for good reason, and its residential neighbourhoods carry a lot of character. The older core — Port Dalhousie, downtown St. Catharines, and the streets between Lakeshore and Geneva — has Victorian and Edwardian homes with ornate trim, deep lots, and the bones of quality construction from over a century ago. Renovating these properties rewards craftsmanship and attention to period detail.

North end and Lakeshore communities feature a mix of mid-century bungalows and split-levels — solid 1950s through 1970s builds that are among the most cost-effective renovation candidates in the Niagara region. The south end has newer suburban homes from the 1980s through 2000s where owners are upgrading beyond builder finishes.

Merritton and Thorold-adjacent areas are seeing growing renovation interest as property values rise and homeowners invest in long-term improvement.
