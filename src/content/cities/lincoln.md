## Lincoln's Residential Landscape

Lincoln is characterized by its blend of rural, agricultural, and residential land use. Villages like Jordan and Vineland have older homes dating back generations — brick farmhouses, century homes with wide lots, and postwar bungalows that have seen multiple renovation cycles. These are heritage-adjacent properties where craftsmanship and attention to original detail matter.

Jordan Station's residential streets have a mix of older and newer homes, many on generous lots that allow for additions or detached garage conversions alongside the primary renovation scope. Campden and the rural stretches of Lincoln feature hobby farms and estate properties where renovation projects are often large-scale.

The newer subdivision development around Vineland and the western portions of Lincoln brings modern builds that need the same upgrades common throughout the Niagara Peninsula: basement finishing, kitchen refreshes, and bathroom modernizations.
