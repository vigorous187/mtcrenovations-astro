## Housing in the Beamsville Area

Beamsville's residential character reflects its agricultural heritage. Older homes in the village core date from the late 1800s through the mid-1900s — brick and wood-frame construction with character details like transom windows, original trim profiles, and deep front porches. These homes respond beautifully to renovations that respect the original scale while adding modern kitchens and bathrooms.

The newer subdivisions on the east and west edges of the village have brought two-storey and bungalow homes from the 1990s and 2000s into the community. These are your standard renovation candidates: unfinished basements, dated kitchens with laminate counters and stock cabinets, and bathrooms that haven't been touched since the home was built.

Rural properties and hobby farms in the surrounding Lincoln township offer their own renovation scope — farmhouse kitchen conversions, barn transformations, and heritage property restorations.
