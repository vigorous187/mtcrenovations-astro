## Niagara Falls Housing Stock

Behind the tourist corridor, Niagara Falls is a real residential city with a rich cross-section of housing types. The older established neighbourhoods near Morrison Street and Dorchester Road feature brick bungalows, two-storey homes, and split-levels from the postwar decades — properties that are well-suited to kitchen overhauls and basement finishing projects.

Stamford Centre and Fernwood are among Niagara Falls' more desirable residential pockets, with larger homes on mature lots. These properties often support more ambitious renovation scopes: full primary suite renovations, open-concept main floor conversions, and high-end basement entertainment rooms.

Chippawa and the south end have a more rural character with properties on larger lots where homeowners are investing in updating older bungalows. The Crowland area and north Niagara Falls have a range of mid-century housing with strong renovation value.
