## Scarborough's Housing Mix

Scarborough offers some of the most varied housing stock in the GTA. Older neighbourhoods like Birchcliff, Cliffside, and Cliffcrest along the bluffs feature 1940s and 1950s bungalows and Cape Cods on large lots — many of these are actively being renovated by second and third owners ready for a full transformation.

The postwar suburban grid of Agincourt, Wexford, and Ionview is filled with brick bungalows and split-levels from the 1950s through 1970s — these are Scarborough's bread-and-butter renovation properties. Basement finishing and kitchen overhauls are the top requests. Malvern, Morningside Heights, and Scarborough Village have larger homes from the 1980s and 1990s where owners are upgrading bathrooms, replacing flooring throughout, and building out full basement suites.
