## Etobicoke's Housing and Renovation Culture

Etobicoke is one of Toronto's most desirable west-end communities, and renovation activity here is high. The postwar bungalows in Long Branch, New Toronto, and Alderwood were built in the late 1940s and 1950s on generously sized lots — many are now being renovated top to bottom as buyers invest heavily in properties under significant upward price pressure.

The Kingsway and Humber Valley Village areas feature larger detached homes from the 1920s through 1940s with distinguished architectural character: Tudor details, limestone finishes, and expansive floor plans. These properties require restoration sensibility alongside modern renovation skills.

Rexdale and Westway offer more affordable bungalows and split-levels from the 1960s — solid stock that benefits enormously from kitchen overhauls, main floor flooring replacements, and basement finishing.
