## Renovating in Toronto

Toronto's housing stock is as diverse as the city itself. Victorian and Edwardian semi-detached homes in the core, post-war bungalows in the inner suburbs, and newer builds in the outer boroughs each present different renovation opportunities.

Basement finishing is in high demand across Toronto, especially in the older homes where unfinished or poorly finished basements represent significant untapped square footage. Kitchen and bathroom renovations are popular in Toronto's 1950s-1970s housing stock, where original layouts and fixtures are overdue for modernization.

With Toronto's real estate values, every renovation dollar counts. A well-executed basement finish or kitchen remodel delivers strong return on investment in this market.
