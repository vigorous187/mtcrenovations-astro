## Guelph's Housing Character

Guelph has a housing market shaped by its university town character and a steady stream of long-term residents with real investment in their properties. The south end near the Speed River and downtown Guelph features some of the oldest residential architecture in Ontario — limestone-clad century homes, Victorian red-brick semis, and Edwardian detached homes with original woodwork details that reward careful renovation work.

The postwar neighbourhoods of Brant, Waverley, and St. George's Park offer solid brick bungalows and two-storeys from the 1950s and 1960s — the backbone of Guelph's renovation market. These properties commonly need kitchen overhauls, bathroom updates, and basement finishing to maximize their livable square footage.

Westminster Woods, Kortright Hills, and the newer south-end subdivisions have homes from the 1990s and 2000s where the priority shifts to basement finishing and upgrading builder-grade finishes in kitchens and bathrooms.
