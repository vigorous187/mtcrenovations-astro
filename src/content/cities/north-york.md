## North York's Diverse Housing

North York has one of the highest concentrations of mid-century housing in Canada. The postwar boom years produced thousands of bungalows, backsplits, sidesplits, and two-storey homes across Willowdale, Don Mills, and Bathurst Manor — most built between 1950 and 1975 by now need significant renovation investment to meet modern standards.

Older neighbourhoods like Lawrence Park, Bedford Park, and Forest Hill North have larger detached homes from the 1920s through 1940s — high-demand properties where owners invest substantially in quality renovations. These projects often involve kitchen extensions, primary bathroom expansions, and full basement rebuilds.

The newer condominium developments along Yonge Street and Sheppard Avenue serve a different clientele — condo renovation projects requiring precise, quiet, schedule-disciplined work that complies with building management rules.
