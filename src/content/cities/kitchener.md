## Kitchener's Renovation Landscape

Kitchener is one of Canada's fastest-growing mid-sized cities, driven by a booming tech sector and strong institutional anchors. That growth translates directly into renovation demand — homeowners with rising property equity and the income to invest meaningfully in their homes.

The older Kitchener neighbourhoods of Rockway, Mill Courtland, and the Kingsdale community have postwar bungalows and split-levels from the 1940s through 1960s that are prime renovation candidates. These homes have never been modernized and offer the best ROI for kitchen overhauls, bathroom upgrades, and basement finishing.

Forest Heights and Westmount are where Kitchener's established professionals settled from the 1960s through 1980s — larger two-storey and sidesplit homes that are now hitting their third renovation cycle. These are the custom kitchen, ensuite spa, and high-end basement projects.

Doon, Huron Park, and Laurentian West are the growth areas — newer homes from the 1990s through 2010s where basement finishing and first-generation upgrades are most common.
