## Markham's Housing Market and Renovation Demand

Markham is one of Canada's most affluent mid-sized cities — and its renovation market reflects that. Homeowners here expect high-quality materials, experienced tradespeople, and project management that keeps things moving. That's exactly what MTC provides.

Unionville's historic core has century homes and Victorian-era properties that attract buyers specifically for their character — and those buyers invest significantly in careful renovations that preserve the original details while adding modern kitchens and bathrooms.

Markham Village and the older sections of Thornhill Road have postwar homes from the 1950s and 1960s that are renovation staples — bungalows and two-storeys with solid bones and strong upgrade potential for kitchen overhauls, basement finishing, and floor replacements.

Cornell, Greensborough, and the Wismer Commons area represent Markham's newer suburban growth — large two-storey and townhome developments from the 2000s and 2010s where first-generation upgrades are now underway. Basement finishing, primary ensuite renovations, and kitchen cabinet-and-countertop refreshes are the most common requests here.
