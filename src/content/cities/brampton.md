## Brampton's Housing Landscape

Brampton is predominantly a city of newer subdivisions, with large portions of the housing stock built between the 1990s and 2010s. Many of these homes feature standard builder finishes that are aging out — laminate countertops, hollow-core doors, basic tile, and unfinished basements that sit unused for years.

Older Brampton neighbourhoods like downtown Brampton and Bramalea have stock from the 1960s and 1970s that often needs more comprehensive updates: kitchen layouts reworked, dated bathrooms brought to modern standards, and basement waterproofing addressed before finishing.

The Mount Pleasant and Credit Valley communities have newer homes where owners are upgrading faster — custom primary bathrooms, high-end kitchen remodels, and finished lower levels with separate entrances for in-law suites or rental income.
